ABOUT KUROKAWA

The Onsen Villiage of Kurokawa lies deep in the valleys of Mt. Aso in Japan.

Engulfed in nature, the area has been protected and preserved by the warm, unaffected local people for a long time and as a result as remained largely unchanged. Row upon row of mountains and beautiful undulating hills stretch into the distance. Shivering silver grass covers the land as far as the eye can see. People have protected the area’s cedar forests for hundreds of years.

Clear blue mountain streams trickle into thundering rivers. Though there are in total 29 independent bath houses and inn that share the hot-spring water that gushes forth from the mountain rock, they are united as one resort under the name “Kurokawa Onsen Inn” and hand-in-hand they have continued to show their utmost hospitality to travellers.

Almost all of the lodges boast their own individual style of open-air bath. However the Inn runs a system where one can use any bath in the area regardless of which ever lodge you happening to be staying at. Each inn or guesthouse in the area has its own long history as a spa and though there individual designs may be subtly different they all offer a collection of the best local produce.

Kurokawa’s people have always cherished they traditions such as Yoshiwara Kagura a dance said to be first performed by god’s, the solemn ritual of Yamanami Daiko, Yayaki, the Jizoudo Hall dedicated to the Buddhist monk of the same name and the practice of controlled burns that has protected the regions meadows for hundreds of years. Through the protection of tradition residents of the area hope to pass down the appreciation of nature that is so important to the Japanese people.

ABOUT KUROKAWAWONDERLAND

This project was collaboration, largely aimed at foreign audiences, between the residents of Kurokawa and Minami Oguni, a town that lies at the foot of Mt. Aso and a group of creators from all kinds of genres and mediums that are currently active in Tokyo.

Through using Kurokawa’s traditions and mythology as a base for a short film, which made up the project’s core, as well web design, photography and music we aimed to express the essence of Kurokawa. Furthermore the project aimed to give each artist complete control over each aspect of their field so that work they made as part of the project could also function as part of their personal portfolio.

Through experiencing Kurokawa’s nature and traditions and above all the process of working with the honest, warm-hearted people of Kurokawa a loving connection between the creators and the region become truly palpable in each aspect of the work that was produced.

Through placing importance on meritocratic principles, minimizing staff numbers we were able to create a highly creative team who complemented each other’s abilities. As a result we were able to create work to an extremely high quality.

Budgeting was left entirely to the discretion of each creator on an individual basis whilst the residents of Kurokawa provided accommodation, amenities and food for the crew whilst on location.

The ultimate goal of this project was to share Kurokawa’s and Japan’s culture and mentality with a foreign audience. On top of this, if this project can assist in the revitalization of the area and make what the area has to offer accessible to a wider audience, we would all be happy.
